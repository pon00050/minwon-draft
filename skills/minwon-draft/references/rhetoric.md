# rhetoric.md — voice, phrasing, and what NOT to write

House style:
> 확신 있는 존댓말 + "첫째/둘째/셋째" 선언적 논거 + "~로 이해됩니다. 이 이해가 맞는지
> 확인 요청드립니다"(assert-then-confirm) + 질문은 "확인 요청드립니다 / 안내하여 주시기
> 바랍니다"로 마무리.

**Non-adversarial ≠ timid.** Non-aggression comes from the framing disclaimer and
one human closing — NOT from weakening sentence endings. Every sentence stays
declarative and confident.

---

## Register & fixed frame
- 하십시오체 only. Open every body with `안녕하십니까.`, close with `감사합니다.`
  (formal 공문 adds `끝.`).
- Self: `본인` / `저` / `신청인`. Agency: `귀 부서` / `귀 위원회` / `귀 공단` / `귀 기관`.
- Numbers, dates, articles are always explicit and absolute — `2026-06-13`,
  `제49조 제3항`, `₩155,000` — never "recently / 얼마 전 / 약간".

## Assert-then-confirm — the exact 문형
> "[진술]…로 이해됩니다. 이 이해가 맞는지 확인 요청드립니다."

Then, only where the answer could go against you, append:
> "만약 이해와 다르다면 그 법적 근거를, 명문 규정이 없어 내부 기준으로 운용되고 있다면
> 그 기준을 함께 안내하여 주시기 바랍니다."

Do **not** append a safety-net softener ("혹시 제가 잘못 이해했다면 편하게 바로잡아
주셔도 좋습니다"). It dissolves the burden-shift. Throw the assertion with
confidence; the rebuttal cost stays with the reader.

### Calibrate to the analytical layer — 법규 / 사실 / 포섭

Before picking a 문형, locate each statement in one of three layers. Posture
follows the layer, not the sentence's grammar — this is precision about *what* you
assert, never a licence to soften.

| Layer | What it is | Posture | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **① 법규 (rule)** | what the statute says | **Assert** — verified, verbatim | "제32조 제1호는 횡단보도ㆍ보도에서의 정차ㆍ주차를 금지합니다." |
| **② 사실 (fact)** | what was observed | **Assert** — anchored to evidence | "첨부 사진은 차량이 횡단보도 노면표시 위에 있음을 보여줍니다." |
| **③ 포섭 (subsumption)** | ①을 ②에 적용한 법적 효과 | **Ask** by default — this is what the agency is being asked to decide | "본 사실관계가 제160조 제3항의 요건을 충족하는지." |

Run the diagnostic — **where does the dispute actually live?**
- **사실이 다투어지는 경우** → the work is *evidentiary*: assert the fact and carry the
  proof (일시ㆍ위치ㆍ번호판ㆍ사진). Do **not** hedge a fact you can document
  ("있는 것으로 보입니다" → "사진상 …위에 있습니다").
- **법적 효과가 다투어지는 경우** (facts accepted, ③ open) → the work is *interpretive*:
  **ask** the subsumption, then protect the systemic question (below).

Conflating ② and ③ is the common error — it makes you either hedge a provable fact
or assert a subsumption the office must determine. Keep them as separate sentences.
The downshift 문형 for ③:
> "본 사실관계에 [법 제○조 제○항]이 적용되는지, 적용된다면 그 요건 충족 여부를 확인 요청드립니다."
This still shifts the burden, but forces a *reasoned interpretation* instead of a
curt "전제가 틀렸습니다" that closes the line.

**Assert a ③ subsumption only when** (a) it is legally indisputable, or (b) you are
running a clearance/advocacy play — assert the *favorable* subsumption and invite
rebuttal because any answer is citable ("…에 해당하지 않는다고 볼 수 있습니까?"). Even then,
never assert it where a one-line premise-denial would foreclose a systemic question
you need answered.

**Protect the systemic question.** When the 민원's real value is an enforcement-gap
or policy question ("이런 유형은 현행법상 어떻게 처리되는가"), never hang it on a contested
premise. Split it: ask the ③ fact-application question, then ask the systemic
question *independently* ("적용이 곤란하다면, 이 유형에 적용되는 집행수단은 무엇입니까"), so a
"no" to the first still yields the gap answer. A denial you *engineered* to be
informative is a win; a denial that ends the conversation is a drafting error.

*(Worked illustration — 이륜차의 횡단보도 불법주차 신고. Weaker: "제160조 제3항에 따라 과태료
부과가 가능한 것으로 이해됩니다" — a contested subsumption an official can deny in one line.
Stronger: "본 사실관계에 제160조 제3항이 적용되는지 확인 요청드립니다" **plus a separate**
"적용이 곤란하다면 이륜차의 횡단보도 불법주차에 적용되는 집행수단은 무엇입니까". The split is what
keeps the enforcement-gap answer alive even under a "부과 불가" reply.)*

## 어미 whitelist (question/request endings)
`~확인 요청드립니다` · `~적용되는지 (요건 충족 여부를) 확인 요청드립니다` ·
`~안내하여 주시기 바랍니다` · `~검토를 요청드립니다` ·
`~서면 회신 부탁드립니다` · `~구별됩니다` · `~해당하지 않는다고 볼 수 있습니까?`

## 금지 표현 (delete on sight)
`죄송합니다` · `조심스럽지만` · `혹시`(빈번한 완곡어법) · `여쭙고 싶습니다` · `궁금합니다` ·
`~해주실 수 있으신지요` · `부담 갖지 마시고` · `바쁘신 중에`. These read as a wavering
tone and invite strict interpretation — the exact opposite of the goal.

## Framing disclaimer (defuse an adversarial reading — near the top, once, firmly)
State what the 민원 is **not**:
> "본 민원은 특정 업소에 대한 처분을 요청하는 개인 구제 신청이 아니라, … 정책 질의입니다."
> "이번 민원은 위 답변이나 특정 판매업소의 위반 여부를 다시 다투려는 것이 아니라, …
> 한 단계 더 나아간 정책적 질문을 드리고자 합니다."

## Human closing (one short paragraph at the very end — optional, for rights/policy/safety 민원)
> "가장 좋은 결과는 처벌이 아니라 더 많은 사람을 포용하는 서비스 설계로의 변화라고
> 생각합니다."
> "이 민원을 드리는 이유는 특정 업소나 담당 부서를 문제 삼기 위해서가 아니라, 저와 같은
> 이용자들이 더 안전하고 포용적인 환경에서 서비스를 이용할 수 있기를 바라는 마음에서입니다."

## Follow-up opener (when replying to a prior answer)
Thank first (even for an evasive reply), then concede the settled points so the
clerk faces only the open ones:
> "성심껏 답변해 주셔서 감사드립니다. … 다음 사항은 이미 명확히 확인되었음을 알려드립니다
> (재확인은 필요하지 않으며, 아래 이해에 오류가 있는 경우에만 지적해 주시기 바랍니다). …
> 다만 아래 두 가지는 확인되지 않아 별도로 서면 확인을 요청드립니다."

## Question engineering (make every answer useful)
- **Does such a rule exist?** Ask whether a limit/standard exists; a "no such
  rule" reply is itself leverage (documented gap, or a shield to hand a third
  party). Or ask "…기준을 신설/개정할 계획이 있는지" — "no plans" documents the gap.
- **Bidirectional.** Draft so each branch maps to a prepared action (this is
  Play 2). If a branch has no favorable move, re-scope the question.
- **절차질문 → 보완민원.** Stage 1: neutral "what review procedure exists for X?"
  Stage 2: whatever comes back ("현행상 불가" included) becomes the premise of a
  supplementary filing. Good for policy-change campaigns whose first answer will
  be unfavorable.
- **Pre-close the rebuttal inside the question.** Supply the purposive reasoning
  yourself so the clerk has less room to punt (e.g. "본 서비스는 자금을 매개·전달하지 않고
  플랫폼이 거래 당사자로 개입하므로, 제2조 제19호가 규율하려는 제3자 정산 대행의 위험이 구조적으로
  발생하지 않습니다. 따라서 … 이해되는데, 맞습니까?").
- **Multiple choice (가/나/다).** Offer an enumerated menu including a "no rule
  prohibits it → provider's discretion" box, so the favorable pick is low-commitment.
- **Scope-restrict to 문언 해석.** "본 질의는 문언 해석에 관한 것으로, 개별 사안 심사(…위탁)와
  구별됩니다." The primary anti-punt device.

## Sensitive-term navigation (avoid triggering the wrong regulator)
When the literal description of an operation maps onto a regulated activity,
recharacterize functionally in neutral, self-serving vocabulary rather than
adopting the trigger term:
- Describe the actor as a **principal (거래 당사자)**, not an intermediary.
- Describe reimbursement as **채권 양수 / 팩토링**, not 정산/결제대행.
- If you must name the sensitive category, do it as "confirm we are NOT it" —
  assert non-applicability with reasons + ask for confirmation.
- If a regulator's prior answer used a loaded word (e.g. "판매"), quote it as
  **their** word in a blockquote and ask whether your neutral facts meet their
  term — never self-apply the label.
- (Domain guardrail example: in a payments-clearance 민원, avoid `결제대행 · 정산 ·
  마켓플레이스`; prefer `거래 당사자 · 채권 양수 · 위임청구` where accurate.)

## Estoppel / consistency pressure
- Quote the agency's **own prior written answer** back to it verbatim, with
  민원번호 + 날짜 + 담당자, so any new answer must stay consistent or visibly contradict.
- Cite a **different** agency's finding to constrain the target (cross-agency
  consistency) — convert each answer into a fixed premise for the next.
- After a written admission of an agency error, frame the next step around 금반언
  ("행정청은 자신의 위법으로 시계를 리셋할 수 없다").
- Cite the agency's own precedent decisions and ask for **유추 적용**, not a new ruling.
- For minister letters: quote the official's own public promise and frame the ask
  as helping them keep it.

## What NOT to write (rules of thumb)
- **G1** Don't grovel. Confident 존댓말; lower defensiveness via framing + closing.
- **G2** Don't over-claim or predict the unprovable. "가능성을 배제할 수 없다" beats
  "반드시 …할 것". 절제가 곧 힘 — understate to stay unrebuttable.
- **G3** Don't cite a source you haven't verified (law.go.kr for statutes; check
  numbers). An unverified citation discredits the whole filing.
- **G4** Don't quote your own favorable snippet without checking the *same*
  document for an adjacent adverse phrase that would strengthen the other side.
- **G5** Don't file a hypothetical/premature question (no standing yet) — you get
  a non-answer. Establish the predicate facts first.
- **G6** Don't re-ask what's already answered — it lowers credibility. Concede it.
- **G7** Lead with your best-evidenced, least-contestable ground; demote arguments
  resting on weak evidence.
- **G8** Scope factual claims tightly ("the only official mall for *this brand*")
  to survive an obvious counterexample.
- **G9** When citing foreign/○○ practice, concede its limits yourself to remove
  the "you're overreaching" rebuttal.
- **G10** Once a formal 처분 exists, informal channels are closed — don't re-email a
  silent official or refile the same thing; escalate to a different track.
